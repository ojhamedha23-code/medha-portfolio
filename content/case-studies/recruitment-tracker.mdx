## The problem

Job searching is chaotic. Applications go into a black hole, follow-up dates get forgotten, and it's hard to get a clear picture of where you stand across 20+ companies at once.

Spreadsheets technically work — but they're ugly, hard to scan, and offer no structure for the specific workflow of a job search.

## What I built

A single-page HTML dashboard that tracks every application from first contact to offer. It's designed to be beautiful enough that you actually want to open it every day.

Key features:
- Track status across every stage (applied, phone screen, interview, offer, rejected)
- Add notes, deadlines, and contacts per company
- Clean, readable layout that doesn't feel like a spreadsheet
- Zero setup — download and open in any browser

## Design decisions

The tracker uses a navy, burgundy, and gold palette to feel professional rather than clinical. Typography is set in Playfair Display and Lato — editorial and readable.

Everything is a single self-contained HTML file. No accounts, no sync, no cloud. Your data stays on your machine.
