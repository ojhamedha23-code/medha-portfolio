## The Problem

Customer support in India runs almost entirely on human agents. For small and medium businesses, this creates a compounding set of problems that are hard to fix with headcount alone:

- **Cost** — Hiring, training, and managing a support team is expensive at every scale. Agent attrition makes it worse.
- **Coverage** — Human agents work shifts. Customers don't. Calls that come in at night, on weekends, or during peak hours either go unanswered or hit long queues.
- **Consistency** — The quality of a customer interaction depends on which agent picks up, how they're feeling, and how well they've been trained. That variability is invisible until it's already cost you a customer.
- **Speed** — A customer with a billing question or delivery issue wants an answer in seconds, not after navigating an IVR tree and waiting on hold.

The opportunity was clear: a voice AI agent that could handle these calls around the clock — in natural, human-like Indian English and Hinglish — would directly address all four.

## What I Built

Adya's AI Voice Agent (the platform we internally called V2V — Voice-to-Voice) is a product that lets businesses deploy custom AI agents for inbound and outbound phone calls. The agent can hold full, context-aware conversations — qualifying leads, resolving support queries, scheduling appointments, gathering feedback, and routing to a human when needed.

The customer-facing platform I designed and shipped included:

**Call Agent Configuration**
Every business has a different script, tone, and set of tasks. I built the system for creating custom agent personas — defining the agent's voice, language, call flow logic, and escalation rules. A logistics company's agent handles delivery rescheduling. A clinic's agent handles appointment booking. The configuration layer made this reusable without engineering involvement for each new client.

**Campaign Management**
For outbound use cases — follow-ups, reminders, lead qualification — we built a campaigns module. Businesses could upload contact lists, assign an agent configuration, schedule a campaign, and get structured results back. Internally, this was the feature that most directly replaced repetitive human outbound calling.

**Supervisor Dashboard**
Managers needed visibility. I designed a real-time dashboard showing active calls, call status, and quality indicators — giving supervisors the ability to monitor live conversations and intervene when needed. Post-call, the platform surfaces AI-generated summaries and sentiment analysis for every call, turning a conversation into a structured record without any manual note-taking.

**Contacts and Groups**
To make campaigns repeatable and targetable, we built a contact management layer — with groups for segmentation and reuse across multiple campaigns.

## My Role

I joined Adya as the product manager for this product and built it from zero to one.

That meant starting with no existing product, no defined spec, and no prior customer reference point. My work spanned the full PM surface:

**Customer Discovery**
I spoke with SMBs across sectors — retail, logistics, clinics, financial services — to map out where phone calls were creating the most friction. The pattern that emerged: high-volume, repetitive calls (order status, appointment reminders, payment follow-ups) were consuming the most agent time and were the most automatable.

**Product Definition**
I translated customer conversations into a product architecture — what the platform needed to do, what customisation options customers needed, and what the minimum viable version looked like for a first deployment.

**UI Design**
I defined the user interface for the customer-facing dashboard — how call agents are configured, how campaigns are set up and tracked, how supervisors monitor live operations, and how post-call analytics are surfaced.

**Custom Agent Deployments**
For each new enterprise client, I worked on the agent configuration — adapting call flows, scripts, and escalation logic to fit their specific use case. This was hands-on product work that also served as the primary touchpoint for customer onboarding.

**Technical Demos**
I ran product demonstrations directly with enterprise prospects — walking through the platform, showing live call examples, and handling technical questions. Being the PM who could also do the demo removed a handoff that would have slowed the sales cycle down significantly.

**Iteration**
Every client deployment surfaced something. I managed the feedback loop across versions — prioritising what changed the call quality, what reduced setup time, and what unlocked new use cases.

## What the Product Does Today

The platform handles the full call lifecycle:

- **Inbound calls** — AI agent picks up, identifies the customer's intent, resolves the query or routes to the right team
- **Outbound campaigns** — Automated calls to contact lists with real-time tracking and structured outcomes per call
- **AI call summaries** — Every call produces a written summary automatically, no agent note-taking required
- **Sentiment analysis** — Post-call sentiment flagging surfaces calls that need follow-up or human review
- **Supervisor view** — Live monitoring of active calls with quality indicators and the ability to intervene

The product documentation is available at [docs.adya.ai](https://docs.adya.ai/s/home/doc/ai-voice-agent-DaIEBcRN4A), and Adya's own [product update announcement](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/adyadotai_ai-voiceai-callautomation-activity-7363224237387468800-qYHu) shows the platform in production.
